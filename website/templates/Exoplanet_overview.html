Exoplanet Types

Gas Giants

These massive planets can have incredibly strong winds and storms, far exceeding anything seen on Jupiter. Some gas giants orbit multiple stars in binary or trinary systems, leading to complex orbital mechanics and potentially unusual atmospheric conditions.

Neptune-Like

The atmospheres of some Neptune-like planets can be incredibly hot, despite their distance from their stars, a phenomenon known as "puffy Neptunes." These planets have unusually large radii for their mass, suggesting extended, low-density atmospheres that are still not fully understood.

Terrestrial

Some rocky exoplanets might be tidally locked to their stars, meaning one side perpetually faces the star (experiencing extreme heat) while the other side is in permanent darkness (experiencing extreme cold). The presence of a significant atmosphere could potentially redistribute heat and make more of the planet habitable.

Super-Earth

The high gravity on some super-Earths could lead to geological activity unlike anything on Earth, such as supervolcanoes that erupt much more violently and frequently. Some super-Earths might also possess global oceans or even be entirely covered in water, sometimes referred to as "ocean planets."
