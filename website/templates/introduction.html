🌌 Introduction to Exoplanet Detection

Welcome to the Exoplanet Detection App, where we explore the fascinating world of exoplanets—planets that orbit stars beyond our solar system. For years, astronomers have been discovering and studying these distant worlds, unraveling the mysteries of the universe. However, identifying exoplanets is a complex task, requiring accurate data analysis and sophisticated tools.

This app uses machine learning to analyze data collected from space missions such as Kepler and TESS, to predict whether a celestial object is likely to be an exoplanet. By using a variety of features, including orbital characteristics, brightness levels, and other astronomical data, our model can determine if an object is a confirmed exoplanet or a false positive.

Whether you're a researcher, a student, or simply a space enthusiast, this app provides a unique opportunity to interact with cutting-edge astronomical data and get a deeper understanding of how exoplanets are discovered. Our goal is to make this process more accessible, allowing you to explore and make predictions on your own.

The Exoplanet Detection App also provides explanations of the various features used to train our machine learning model. These features play a critical role in predicting whether a star-planet system contains a potentially habitable world, a discovery that could have profound implications for the search for life beyond Earth.
