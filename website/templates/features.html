🧬 Feature Descriptions

  • Not Transit-like (koi_fpflag_nt): Shows if the signal is not from a planet.
    Values: 0 or 1
    Meaning: 1 means the signal is likely NOT a planet transit.
    Use: Helps exclude signals that don’t match expected planet-like patterns.
  • Stellar Binary Signature (koi_fpflag_ss): Identifies likely binary star eclipses.
    Values: 0 or 1
    Meaning: 1 means the event may be caused by a star eclipsing another.
    Use: Filters out stellar binary systems from planet candidates.
  • Centroid Offset (koi_fpflag_co): Shows signal offset from target.
    Values: 0 or 1
    Meaning: 1 suggests signal may come from nearby background object.
    Use: Removes false positives caused by misaligned signal sources.
  • Ephemeris Match (koi_fpflag_ec): Checks for duplicate timing.
    Values: 0 or 1
    Meaning: 1 means signal timing overlaps with known sources.
    Use: Flags data confusion or blend contamination.
  • Orbital Period (koi_period): Days to complete one orbit.
    Values: Decimal (e.g., 3.141)
    Meaning: Time a planet takes to orbit its host star.
    Use: Important for determining planetary year and potential habitability.
  • First Transit Time (koi_time0bk): Central time of first observed transit.
    Values: BKJD (e.g., 130.245)
    Meaning: Kepler Barycentric Julian Date of first transit.
    Use: Reference point for calculating future transit events.
  • Impact Parameter (koi_impact): Closest approach of the planet.
    Values: 0 to 1
    Meaning: Describes the distance from star center during transit.
    Use: Helps model transit shape and inclination.
  • Transit Duration (koi_duration): How long the transit lasts.
    Values: Decimal hours (e.g., 4.75)
    Meaning: Duration the planet blocks the star.
    Use: Helps determine planet size and orbit geometry.
  • Transit Depth (koi_depth): Brightness drop during transit.
    Values: ppm (parts per million)
    Meaning: Indicates how much light the planet blocks.
    Use: Estimates planet size relative to its star.
  • Planet Radius (koi_prad): Radius of the planet.
    Values: Earth radii (e.g., 1.2)
    Meaning: Estimated size of the planet.
    Use: Critical for classification as Earth-like or gas giant.
  • Equilibrium Temperature (koi_teq): Estimated temperature.
    Values: Kelvin (K)
    Meaning: Temperature assuming no atmosphere or greenhouse effect.
    Use: Helps assess planet's habitability.
  • Insolation Flux (koi_insol): Received stellar energy.
    Values: Earth units (1 = same as Earth)
    Meaning: Amount of sunlight a planet receives.
    Use: Essential for estimating surface conditions.
  • Model SNR (koi_model_snr): Transit signal clarity.
    Values: Decimal (e.g., 10.5)
    Meaning: Signal-to-noise ratio of the transit model.
    Use: Higher values mean more confident detections.
  • TCE Planet Number (koi_tce_plnt_num): Candidate number in the system.
    Values: Integer
    Meaning: ID number if multiple planets exist around one star.
    Use: Tracks which planet is being referenced in a system.
  • Star Temperature (koi_steff): Surface temperature of the star.
    Values: Kelvin
    Meaning: Effective temperature of the star’s surface.
    Use: Used to understand the star-planet environment.
  • Surface Gravity (koi_slogg): Logarithmic gravity value.
    Values: log10(cm/s²)
    Meaning: Indicates gravitational pull on the star’s surface.
    Use: Helps model stellar size and structure.
  • Stellar Radius (koi_srad): Radius of the star.
    Values: Solar radii
    Meaning: Size of the host star.
    Use: Needed to scale planet and transit features.
  • Kepler Magnitude (koi_kepmag): Brightness of the star.
    Values: Decimal magnitude
    Meaning: How bright the star is in the Kepler band.
    Use: Helps determine observation quality and distance.
  • Data Release Name (koi_tce_delivname): Kepler data version.
    Values: 0 or 1, only one value should be 1 and rest as 0
    Meaning: Which Kepler release the data came from.
    Use: Tracks updates and improvements in analysis.
